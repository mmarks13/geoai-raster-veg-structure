# SAN DIEGO STATE UNIVERSITY FORMATTING GUIDELINES
_SDSU Template, Version 11.1_

1. **Names & Degree Title**: Your name, committee members, and degree title must match University records.

2. **Department Guidelines**: See your department guidelines for your approved reference style guide and other department-specific requirements.

3. **Margins (recommended)**: Set margins to **1.25 in** (left) and **1.0 in** (right, top, bottom). Margins may be reduced to **0.875 in** all around to accommodate oversized tables or figures.

4. **Font & Size**: Use a professional font in **12 pt**. Do not use ornamental/script/cursive/calligraphic fonts. For tables, footnotes, or material outside of the main text, you may use **8 pt** minimum. Use **black** for all text apart from figures, tables, and computer code, which may include color.

5. **Line Spacing**: **1.5** is preferred; **2.0** (double-spacing) is acceptable, except for tables, lists, block quotations, footnotes/endnotes, figure captions, table titles, and bibliographic entries, which must be **single-spaced**.

6. **Section Starts**: Start the first page of each section (Acknowledgements, Table of Contents, Lists, etc.), chapter, and appendix on a **new page** and increase the **top margin to 2 inches** on that page.

7. **Bibliography/References**: Place at the **end of each chapter** _or_ at the **end of the main document text** (but **before** the Appendices), per your department’s style guidelines. Use **single-spacing**.

8. **Lists of Figures/Tables**: If including more than one figure, table, or illustration, create a **separate list** for each type (e.g., List of Figures, List of Tables) including the number, caption/title, and the starting page number.

9. **Labeling & Numbering**: Label tables, figures, illustrations, etc. **uniquely** and number each **consecutively** throughout the document.

10. **Images & Supplementary Materials**: Recommended image resolution is **300 dpi**. Supplementary materials (audio, video, oversized tables/figures) must be submitted to **Montezuma Publishing** at the same time the thesis is submitted.

11. **Table of Contents**: List each **chapter number**, **chapter title**, and the **page** on which each chapter begins. Type the word **“Chapter”** as a heading before listing the numbers and titles.
   - See example:  
     - <https://www.montezumapublishing.com/portals/0/docs/Table%201.1.pdf>  
     - <https://www.montezumapublishing.com/portals/0/docs/Sample_TOC_2015.pdf>

12. **Preliminary Page Numbers**: Use **lowercase Roman numerals**, starting with **page iii** (preferably centered at the bottom).

13. **Order of Preliminary Pages** (first numbered page must be **page iii**):
    - **Title page**: Mandatory; **no page number**
    - **Signature page**: Mandatory; **no page number**
    - **Copyright page**: Mandatory; **numbered iii**
    - **Dedication**: Optional; must have page number
    - **Epigraph**: Optional; must have page number
    - **Abstract**: Mandatory; up to **350 words**, **single-spaced**; must have page number
    - **Table of Contents**: Mandatory; must have page number
    - **List of Tables**, **List of Figures**, etc.: Mandatory when the document includes **more than one** table, figure, etc. (e.g., if you have 2 tables, include a List of Tables; 2 figures, include a List of Figures); must have page number
    - **List of Abbreviations/Acronyms/Symbols**: Optional; must have page number
    - **Preface**: Optional; must have page number
    - **Acknowledgements**: Optional; must have page number _(Department of Biology guidelines differ)_
